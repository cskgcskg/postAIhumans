## The Question

> If intelligence — logic, coding, synthesis, data analysis, and creative drafting — becomes a utility like electricity, available to everyone at near-zero marginal cost, **what are humans for?**

This is a single-file interactive exploration of that question. No frameworks. No build tools. Just open `index.html`.

---

## What's Inside

**Five sections of analysis:**

1. **Skill Displacement Timeline** — What gets commoditised at 2, 5, 10, 20, and 30-year horizons. Not the hype version — the one that accounts for institutional lag.

2. **The Human Premium** — Four categories of irreplaceable human value: high-stakes discernment, complex empathy, physicality, and curated taste. Expandable deep-dives on each.

3. **Cognitive Labour vs. Human Agency** — An animated comparison table tracking the value crossover across three decades.

4. **The Agentic Self** — Six psychological traits required to move from worker to orchestrator. (Spoiler: prompt engineering isn't one of them for long.)

5. **Contrarian Skills** — Five things that seem useless now but become high-value when synthetic intelligence is everywhere: boredom tolerance, deep philosophy, physical handcraft, narrative intuition, and institutional memory.

**Analyst Notes** throughout offer opinionated commentary on the underlying dynamics — the parts that don't fit neatly into frameworks.

## Core Thesis

The economic premium is migrating from **production** to **direction**. Not from "doing" to "thinking" — AI already thinks. From thinking to **caring**: having stakes, having skin in the game, having a body and a community and something to lose.

The final human premium is **character**.
